Cauda Equina Syndrome Screen

Assessing Cauda Equina Syndrome (CES)

Ask specifically about subjective changes in function. Symptoms are often vague in early stages (CESI - Incomplete).

  • Bladder: Difficulty initiating flow, poor stream, loss of sensation of flow, or incontinence (late sign).
  • Bowels: Incontinence or loss of sensation of rectal fullness/passing wind.
  • Sexual Function: Change in erection/ejaculation or vaginal sensation.
  • Saddle Sensation: Numbness when wiping or sitting.

Passive SLR

Straight Leg Raise Test (L4-S1 Nerve Root Tension)

Goal: Test for mechanical tension of the lumbosacral nerve roots.

Technique: Patient supine. Passive hip flexion with knee extended.

Positive Result: Reproduction of patient's familiar leg pain (radicular) between 30-70¬∞ of elevation. Back pain alone is negative.

Sensitisation: If pain occurs, lower leg slightly until pain eases, then Dorsiflex foot. Return of pain confirms neural tissue origin.
